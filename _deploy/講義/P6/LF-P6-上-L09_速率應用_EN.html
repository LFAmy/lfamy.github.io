2. Core Knowledge (15 min)
WHY BOX: Why speed problems?
Speed problems appear in SSPA every year and are essential life skills. Three classic types: 1) Catch-up problems (追及) 2) Meeting problems (相遇) 3) Round-trip average speed.
Key strategy: Draw a timeline! Label each segment with distance, time, and speed.
💡 Catch-up: Time = Distance Gap ÷ Speed Difference. Meeting: Time = Total Distance ÷ Speed Sum. Always draw a timeline first!
📝 Example 1
A leaves at 8am, 60km/h. B leaves at 9am, 80km/h, same direction. When does B catch A?
Solution: Gap = 60×1 = 60km. Speed diff = 20km/h. Time = 60÷20 = 3h. B catches A at 12pm.
📝 Example 2
Two cities 300km apart. Car A: 70km/h east. Car B: 80km/h west. When do they meet?
Solution: Speed sum = 150km/h. Time = 300÷150 = 2 hours.
📝 Example 3
Go: 50km/h, Return: 30km/h (same 150km route). Average speed?
Solution: Go time: 3h. Return time: 5h. Total: 300km÷8h = 37.5km/h.
