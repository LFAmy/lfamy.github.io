II. Core Knowledge + Worked Examples
KP1: Decimal ÷ Integer SSPA
Calculation Steps (column method):
The decimal point in the quotient must align with the decimal point in the dividend.
If there is a remainder, append "0" to the remainder and continue dividing.
When the dividend digit is smaller than the divisor, write "0" in the quotient — never skip a place.
Key memory point: "Quotient decimal point = dividend decimal point"
Trap-Detonating Example (the most important demonstration of this lesson)
Calculate: 7.5 ÷ 5 = ?
Common Error (60% of students)
7.5 ÷ 5 = 15
Forgot to place the decimal point in the quotient — 7.5 ÷ 5 = 1.5, not 15
Correct Method
7.5 ÷ 5 = 1.5
5 × 1 = 5, remainder 2.5; 5 × 0.5 = 2.5, quotient = 1.5
Mnemonic: "The quotient's decimal point aligns with the dividend's; if there is a remainder, append a zero and keep dividing."
(Cantonese original: 「商的小數點，對齊被除數；餘數補個零，繼續除落去」)
Most Frequent Error: Forgetting to place the decimal point in the quotient. Remember: 7.5 ÷ 5 = 1.5, NOT 15!
Second Most Frequent Error: Forgetting to append 0 to the remainder and continue dividing, leading to an inaccurate quotient.

KP2: Decimal ÷ Decimal (Synchronised Shifting Method) SSPA Must-Know
Three-Step Synchronised Shifting Method:
When the divisor is a decimal, use the property that multiplying the dividend and divisor by the same number preserves the quotient:
Divisor has 1 decimal place → multiply both numbers by 10
Divisor has 2 decimal places → multiply both numbers by 100
Divisor has 3 decimal places → multiply both numbers by 1000
Essence: Convert "decimal ÷ decimal" into "integer ÷ integer" for calculation.
Example
Ex3: 3.6 ÷ 1.2 = ? (Divisor has 1 decimal place → multiply both by 10: 36 ÷ 12)
Example
Ex4: 0.75 ÷ 0.15 = ? (Divisor has 2 decimal places → multiply both by 100: 75 ÷ 15)

KP3: Remainder Decimal Point Placement — "Catastrophe" Special Drill SSPA Ultimate Trap
Core Rule (extremely error-prone!):
After synchronised shifting, the remainder's decimal point = the dividend's original decimal point position

E.g.: 1.5 ÷ 0.4 → synchronised × 10 → 15 ÷ 40
15 ÷ 40 = 0 remainder 15 (but this is the shifted remainder!)
Restore: remainder 15 must be ÷ 10 → the true remainder is 1.5

Or calculate directly by column method: 1.5 ÷ 0.4 = 3 remainder 0.3
Check: 0.4 × 3 + 0.3 = 1.2 + 0.3 = 1.5 ✓
Catastrophe Example
Ex5: 2.3 ÷ 0.6 = ? (Find the quotient and remainder)
Catastrophe (85% of students wrong)
2.3 ÷ 0.6 = 3 rem 5
After synchronised × 10: 23 ÷ 6 = 3 rem 5, forgot to ÷ 10 to restore the remainder!
Correct Answer
2.3 ÷ 0.6 = 3 rem 0.5
Check: 0.6 × 3 + 0.5 = 1.8 + 0.5 = 2.3 ✓
Example
Ex6: 3.7 ÷ 0.8 = ? (Find the quotient and remainder)
Mnemonic: "After shifting and dividing, restore the remainder; the remainder follows the original decimal point. Verify by multiplying quotient by divisor and adding the remainder — without checking, disaster strikes."
(Cantonese: 「移位計完要還原，餘數跟返原小數點；驗算乘除加餘數，唔驗算實出事」)
Catastrophe: After synchronised shifting, the remainder must be restored! When shifting by ×10/×100, the remainder must be ÷10/÷100 to restore!

#Error PointCorrect Approach
1Misplaced decimal point in quotient: Writing 7.5 ÷ 5 as 15 instead of 1.5Align the quotient's decimal point with the dividend's decimal point
2Shifting only the divisor: For 3.6 ÷ 1.2, only multiplying 1.2 by 10Both dividend and divisor must be shifted synchronously (multiplied by the same factor)
3Forgetting to restore the remainder after shifting (catastrophe): Writing 2.3 ÷ 0.6 as 3 rem 5Divide the remainder by the shift factor to restore it, or verify by checking
4Forgetting to write "0" in the quotient when the digit is too small: For 1.2 ÷ 5, the quotient should be 0.24, not 24Every place must have a quotient digit; write 0 when the dividend digit is too small
5Not appending 0 to continue dividing: 7 ÷ 4 = 1.75, not 1 rem 3When there is a remainder, append 0 and continue dividing until the division terminates or the specified number of decimal places is reached
6Forgetting unit conversion in application problems: Mixing dollars and cents, metres and centimetresUnify all units before calculation
7Missing answer statement / missing remainder in application problems: Only writing the quotient, not "how much remains"A complete answer statement includes both the quotient and the remainder; otherwise method marks are deducted
