2. Core Knowledge (15 min)
WHY BOX: Why integrate?
Real problems don't come labeled 'this is a fraction problem'! SSPA tests your ability to recognize which concept to apply. Integrated problems mix fractions, decimals, percentages, and basic arithmetic.
Strategy: Read the problem → identify the math concept → apply the right formula. Drawing diagrams helps!
💡 Integrated Problem Strategy: 1) Find the 'base unit' (基準量) 2) Determine if you're finding a 'part' or the 'whole' 3) Draw a bar model for visualization.
📝 Example 1
A shirt costs $200. Store A offers 30% off. Store B offers 1/3 off. Which is cheaper?
Solution: Store A: 200×0.7=$140. Store B: 200×(2/3)=$133.33. Store B is cheaper.
📝 Example 2
John spent 2/5 of his money on food and 0.25 on transport. He has $70 left. How much did he start with?
Solution: Spent: 2/5+0.25=0.4+0.25=0.65. Remaining: 0.35=$70. Total: 70÷0.35=$200.
📝 Example 3
A tank is 40% full. Adding 30L makes it 70% full. What is the tank's capacity?
Solution: 30L = 30% of capacity. Capacity = 30÷0.3 = 100L.
