2. Core Knowledge (15 min)
WHY BOX: Why averages and speed?
Average ≠ 'add two numbers and divide by 2'! Average = total sum ÷ count. Speed = distance ÷ time. These are fundamental life skills — from calculating your test score average to estimating travel time.
Trap: Average speed ≠ average of speeds! You must use total distance ÷ total time.
💡 Average = Total÷Count. Speed = Distance÷Time. Average Speed = Total Distance÷Total Time (not average of speeds!)
📝 Example 1
Scores: 85, 92, 78, 95. Average?
Solution: (85+92+78+95)÷4 = 87.5
📝 Example 2
120km in 2 hours. Average speed?
Solution: 120÷2 = 60 km/h
📝 Example 3
Go: 60km/h for 2h. Return: 40km/h for 3h. Average speed?
Solution: Total: 120+120=240km. Time: 2+3=5h. Avg: 240÷5=48km/h. (NOT (60+40)÷2=50!)
