2. Core Knowledge (15 min)
WHY BOX: Why successive changes?
A price goes up 20% then down 20% — is it back to original? NO! $100→$120→$96. Each change applies to a new base!
This is one of the most counterintuitive concepts in math — and a favorite SSPA trap.
Formula: New = Original × (1±r1) × (1±r2) × ... Never add/subtract percentages directly!
💡 Critical Rule: NEVER add percentages for successive changes! Each change applies to a DIFFERENT base. Always multiply: New = Original × (1±r₁) × (1±r₂) × ...
📝 Example 1
Price: $200. Up 10%, then down 10%. Final price?
Solution: $200 × 1.1 × 0.9 = $198. NOT back to $200!
📝 Example 2
Stock: $50. Down 20% then up 25%. Net change?
Solution: $50 × 0.8 × 1.25 = $50. Net change: 0% (surprise — 0.8×1.25=1!)
📝 Example 3
Population: 1000. +5% year 1, +8% year 2. Year 2 population?
Solution: 1000 × 1.05 × 1.08 = 1134 people. Total growth ≠ 5+8=13%!
