2. Core Knowledge (15 min)
WHY BOX: Why circles?
Circles are everywhere — wheels, coins, pizzas, clocks! Two formulas you must NEVER confuse: Circumference = 2πr or πd (length around). Area = πr² (space inside).
Key: Use π=22/7 when radius is a multiple of 7. Otherwise use π=3.14. Always check which one the question expects!
💡 C=2πr (circumference) vs A=πr² (area) — 'C for Circle-around, A for Area-inside'. Never mix them up!
📝 Example 1
Circle radius=7cm. Find circumference and area.
Solution: C=2×22/7×7=44cm. A=22/7×7×7=154cm².
📝 Example 2
Circle diameter=10cm. Find circumference.
Solution: C=π×10=31.4cm.
📝 Example 3
A circular garden has radius 14m. What is its area? (π=22/7)
Solution: A=22/7×14×14=616m².
