🪤 Trap detonation example question 1 (T1 multi-digit - place value trap, SSPA required test every year)
In the number 4,567,890, where is "6"? What is its value?
❌ Common mistakes (65% students)
"6" in "one hundred thousand"
Counting from the left – wrong! Place values ​​are counted from the right. The correct answer is: 5th digit from the right = 10,000 digits
✅ Correct answer
"6" in "ten thousand" → value = 60,000
Counting from the right: 0=pieces, 9=tens, 8=hundreds, 7=thousands, 6=ten thousand, 5=hundreds of thousands, 4=millions
Example 2 - Estimation Trap (T1 - Inconsistent positioning)
Estimate: 486 + 319 ≈ ? (rounded to the nearest hundred)
❌ Common mistakes
500 + 319 = 819 ≈ 800
One has taken the hundreds place (500), the other has not taken it (319) - both must have the same place value!
✅ Correct approach
486≈500, 319≈300 → 500+300 = 800
First take the divisor of each (take the hundreds place together), and then calculate
🧠 Tip: "Count the place value from the right, estimate the place first, add and subtract with the same accuracy, and multiply and divide compatible numbers."
⚠️ T1 The most frequent trap: place value counting from the left vs. counting from the right - always start counting from the right (ones digit)!
⚠️ T1 second high frequency: use "＝" instead of "≈" for estimation, 1 point will be deducted for the test!

🪤 Trap detonation example question 3 (T9 - direct addition of different denominators, the most serious penalty for SSPA subtests)
calculate:23 + 14 = ？
❌ Fatal errors (source of 80% of SSPA points lost)
23 + 14 = 37
Add numerator to numerator (2+1=3), add denominator to denominator (3+4=7)—all wrong!
✅ Correct solution (three-step method)
LCM(3,4)=12 → 812 + 312 = 1112
Simultaneous expansion of the numerator: 2×4=8, 1×3=3 → Add the numerators without changing the denominator

Knowledge Point 3: Review of Decimal Traps 🔴 T9 Traps
Comprehensive review of high-frequency traps in decimal operations:
① Decimal point alignment errors |||SEP|||: The decimal points are not aligned when adding and subtracting - tenths to tenths, hundredths to hundredthsError in the number of decimal places in multiplication |||SEP|||: The decimal place of the product = the sum of the decimal places of each factor (0.3 × 0.4 = 0.12)
② The decimal point shift is inconsistent in division |||SEP|||: The divisor and the dividend must be shifted synchronously (multiplied by 10/100/1000)Decimal comparison only looks at the number but not the place value |||SEP|||: 0.8 > 0.75 (not 0.75 > 0.8! The latter does not mean that the latter has more decimal places)
③ Wrong denominator when converting fractions to decimals(two decimal places → denominator 100), not
④ Forgot to add zeros when multiplying/dividing decimals: 0.8 > 0.75 (not 0.75 > 0.8! More decimal places in the latter does not mean large)
⑤ Wrong denominator when converting fractions to decimals：0.35 = 35100(two decimal places → denominator 100), no3510
⑥ Forgot to add zeros when multiplying/dividing decimals：0.4 × 0.5 = 0.20 = 0.2
🪤 Trap detonation example 5 (T9 - decimal multiplication digit trap)
Calculation: 0.3 × 0.4 = ?
❌ Common mistakes (45% students)
0.3 × 0.4 = 0.12
This is right! The trap is: 0.3 × 0.4 ≠ 0.12? wrong! The real trap is for students to write 0.3×0.4=1.2 (forgetting two decimal places)
✅ Correct rules
3×4=12 → 0.3×0.4=0.12
Factors each have 1 decimal place, 2 in total → product 2 digits from the right plus decimal point: 12 → 0.12
Example 6 - Decimal point shift trap in decimal division
Calculation: 3.6 ÷ 0.12 = ?
❌ Common mistakes
3.6 ÷ 0.12 = 0.3
Just change 3.6 to 36, forget about 0.12 and shift it synchronously!
✅ Correct approach
Divisor×100 → 0.12×100=12
Dividend×100 → 3.6×100=360
360÷12 = 30
Shift both sides simultaneously: the divisor has 2 decimal places → multiply both sides by 100
🧠 Tip: "Align the points for addition and subtraction of decimals, add and bury the number of multiplication digits, and move both sides of division simultaneously. It is better not to move only one side."
⚠️ T9 The most common trap with decimals: Forgetting to calculate the decimal places in multiplication - 0.3×0.5=0.15 (not 1.5! Not 0.015!)
⚠️ T2 order of operations trap: when decimals are mixed, multiply and divide first, then add and subtract, parentheses have the highest priority!
