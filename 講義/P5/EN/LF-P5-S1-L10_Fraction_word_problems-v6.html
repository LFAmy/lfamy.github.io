#common errorCorrect Approach
1"What fraction is left" is directly multiplied by the original total amountCalculate the true remaining amount first, then use the remaining amount × fraction. Baseline amounts may vary each time!
2Skip intermediate calculations in multi-step problemsCalculate step by step, record "how much is used" and "how much is left" every time, without skipping steps.
3"A's C/B" uses division incorrectly"'s" = multiplication: A × C/B. Division is only used if "A is a fraction of B".
4Wrong direction when reasoning backwardsReduction formula: original quantity = remaining quantity ÷ (1 − fraction used). Not a ride!
5Calculate mixed numbers without converting them to improper fractionsFor any multiplication or division involving mixed numbers, the first step = converting improper fractions.
6Unit confusion – fractions vs actual quantities"1/3" may be a fraction (without unit) or an actual quantity (with unit), please read the context clearly!
7Missing sentences/missing units/missing calculations in application questionsMust write "Answer:..." + unit. Calculation: Amount used in all steps + final remaining amount = total amount.
